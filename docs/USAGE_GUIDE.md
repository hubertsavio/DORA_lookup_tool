## Getting Started

### Accessing the Tool
1. Navigate to the application URL
2. Read the disclaimer banner (always visible)
3. Use the search bar on the main page

### First-Time Users
- Review the "About" page to understand the tool's purpose and limitations
- Familiarize yourself with the legal hierarchy (Level 1, 2, 3, Supporting)

## Search Features

### Basic Search
1. Enter your search query in the main search bar
2. Click "Search" or press Enter
3. Results appear grouped by relevance

**Example Queries:**
- "ICT incident reporting"
- "DORA requirements for subcontracting"
- "CTPP oversight"
- "Level 2 measures for ICT risk management"
- "operational resilience testing"

### Natural Language Queries
The tool supports natural language queries:
- "What are the DORA rules for incident reporting?"
- "Show me guidance on third-party risk management"
- "Find all documents about TLPT"

### Search Tips
- Use specific terms for better results
- Include acronyms when relevant (ICT, CTPP, TLPT, RTS, ITS)
- Combine topic areas with document types
- Use quotes for exact phrases

## Filtering Options

### Available Filters

#### 1. Legal Level
- **Level 1 - Primary Legislation**: Binding EU regulations and directives
- **Level 2 - Technical Standards**: RTS, ITS, Delegated Regulations
- **Level 3 - Guidelines**: Non-binding guidance
- **Supporting Material**: Q&As, reports, opinions

#### 2. Document Type
- Regulation
- Directive
- RTS (Regulatory Technical Standard)
- ITS (Implementing Technical Standard)
- Delegated Regulation
- Guideline
- Q&A
- Opinion
- Report
- Other

#### 3. Binding Status
- **Binding Law**: Directly applicable legal requirements
- **Technical Standard (Binding)**: Binding technical specifications
- **Guidance (Non-binding)**: Supervisory guidance
- **Interpretive Material**: Q&As and clarifications
- **Informational**: Background and context

#### 4. Source Body
- EIOPA (European Insurance and Occupational Pensions Authority)
- EBA (European Banking Authority)
- ESMA (European Securities and Markets Authority)
- ESAs (Joint)
- European Commission
- European Parliament
- Council of the EU

#### 5. Topics
- ICT Risk Management
- ICT Third-Party Risk Management
- Digital Operational Resilience Testing
- ICT-Related Incidents
- Information Sharing
- Oversight of Critical Third-Party Providers

#### 6. Toggle Options
- **Include Q&A Documents**: Show/hide Q&A materials
- **Include Oversight-Only Materials**: Show/hide CTPP oversight content

### Using Filters

1. Click the "Filters" button
2. Select desired filter options
3. Filters apply automatically to search results
4. Active filters are displayed as badges
5. Click "Clear All" to reset filters

### Filter Combinations
Combine multiple filters for precise results:
- Level 2 + RTS + ICT Risk Management
- Binding Law + EIOPA + Incident Reporting
- Guidelines + Third-Party Risk

## Understanding Results

### Result Card Components

Each result displays:
1. **Title**: Document name with link to details
2. **Badges**: 
   - Legal level (color-coded)
   - Binding status
   - Document type
   - Special flags (Q&A, Oversight Only)
3. **Summary**: Plain-language overview
4. **Metadata**:
   - Source body
   - Publication date
   - Topics
5. **Warnings**: For non-binding documents
6. **Related Documents**: Cross-references (expandable)
7. **Actions**: View details, official source link

### Badge Color Coding
- 🔴 **Red**: Level 1 / Binding Law
- 🟡 **Yellow**: Level 2 / Technical Standards
- 🔵 **Blue**: Level 3 / Guidelines
- ⚪ **Gray**: Supporting / Informational

### Interpreting Binding Status

**Binding Law**
- Must be followed
- Creates legal obligations
- Directly enforceable

**Technical Standard (Binding)**
- Binding technical specifications
- Detailed implementation requirements
- Legally enforceable

**Guidance (Non-binding)**
- Supervisory expectations
- Best practices
- Not legally binding but influential

**Interpretive Material**
- Clarifications and Q&As
- Helps understand requirements
- Not legally binding

**Informational**
- Background context
- Reports and studies
- No legal force

## Best Practices

### Research Workflow
1. **Start Broad**: Begin with general topic search
2. **Apply Filters**: Narrow down by legal level or type
3. **Review Results**: Read summaries and check binding status
4. **Verify Sources**: Always check official documents
5. **Consult Legal**: Seek legal advice for compliance decisions

### Effective Searching
- Start with topic keywords
- Add specificity gradually
- Use filters to refine results
- Check related documents for context
- Note publication dates for currency

### Understanding Hierarchy
- Level 1 overrides lower levels
- Technical standards implement Level 1
- Guidelines interpret but don't create obligations
- Q&As provide clarification only

### Compliance Research
1. Identify applicable Level 1 requirements
2. Review relevant Level 2 technical standards
3. Consider Level 3 guidance
4. Check Q&As for clarifications
5. **Always consult legal counsel**

### Important Disclaimers

**Not Legal Advice**
This tool provides information for research purposes only. It does not constitute legal advice or create an attorney-client relationship.

**No Compliance Certification**
The tool does not assess, certify, or guarantee compliance with DORA requirements.

**Verify Official Sources**
Always verify information with official EU regulatory sources. This tool may not reflect the most recent updates.

**Ambiguity Warnings**
When regulatory text is ambiguous, the tool indicates this explicitly. Seek legal interpretation for unclear requirements.

**Authority Hierarchy**
The tool respects the legal hierarchy: primary law > technical standards > guidelines > interpretive materials.
